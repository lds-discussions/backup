Issues with the Book of Mormon

In this section, we aim to provide a comprehensive discussion of the issues surrounding the Book of Mormon and how they raise questions about its authenticity, as well as Joseph Smith's role as a prophet of God. We will explore various aspects of the Book of Mormon to present a fuller picture of both the problems related to its scriptures and the common apologetic responses, examining where these defenses are fair and where they fall short.

Overview of Book of Mormon Topics

Part 1: Joseph Smith and Treasure Digging

We begin by examining Joseph Smith's involvement in treasure digging and how his magical worldview influenced both the acquisition of the Book of Mormon plates and the translation process using a seer stone (or "peep stone") in a hat—just as he claimed to use for locating buried treasure.

Part 2: The Gold Plates

Next, we take a closer look at the gold plates from which the Book of Mormon was said to be translated. We explore various accounts to assess how treasure digging affected key elements of the gold plates narrative, including the visitation by a guardian spirit, the retrieval of the plates, and the creation of the "spectacles" or "Nephite Interpreters" during the visit to Hill Cumorah.

Part 3: The Translation

We continue with an examination of the translation process of the Book of Mormon, discussing how treasure digging influenced the procedure. We'll address key issues that arise from the translation accounts, including the heavy reliance on the King James Bible, and the fact that the gold plates were never actually used in the creation of the Book of Mormon as we have it today.

Part 4: The Lost 116 Pages

In this section, we explore the episode of the lost 116 pages of the Book of Mormon, analyzing the event itself, Joseph Smith's subsequent revelation (D&C 10) regarding the "small plates" of Nephi, the vagueness of the replacement text, and the insights the dictation order provides about the authorship of the Book of Mormon.

Part 5: DNA and the Lamanites

We delve into DNA studies to investigate what they reveal about the Book of Mormon's claims of being a literal, historical record. This section considers both the scientific findings and the text of the Book of Mormon, as well as Joseph Smith's revelations concerning the Lamanites and whether other populations could have absorbed the Lehites, as current apologetic arguments suggest.

Part 6: Surrounding Influences

This section discusses external influences that shaped the text of the Book of Mormon. These include Joseph Smith's treasure digging, the King James Bible (previously addressed), the Moundbuilder myth, Freemasonry, Joseph Smith Sr.'s dreams, and contemporary events such as the visit to Charles Anthon and the loss of the 116 pages.

Part 7: Anachronisms

Here, we examine how anachronisms and biblical scholarship help scholars date the Book of Mormon to the 19th century. We discuss the significant reliance on the King James Bible and point out elements that should be present in the Book of Mormon but are missing.

Part 8: Tight vs. Loose Translation Theories

As we near the end of our discussion, we compare the two primary translation theories—tight translation versus loose translation. We analyze which problems arise with each theory and consider testimonies from those who witnessed Joseph Smith's translation process.

Part 9: How the Book of Mormon was Composed

In the final part of our overview, we explore the composition of the Book of Mormon. Using Jacob 5 and the allegory of the olive tree as a case study, we highlight where the author of the Book of Mormon appears to have drawn from external sources, identifying clues that suggest when and by whom the text was written.
